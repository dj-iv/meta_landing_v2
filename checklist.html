The 5-Question Diagnostic

Walk through your office and check each area. Be honest — even one "yes" means you have a problem worth fixing.

1. The Basement or Ground Floor Do you get zero bars, dropped calls, or "No Service" in basement meeting rooms, storage areas, or ground floor spaces?
2. Interior Offices and Meeting Rooms Do staff complain about poor signal in interior offices, conference rooms, or anywhere away from windows?
3. The Stairwells and Elevators Do calls drop when walking between floors, or is there no signal in stairwells and lift areas?
4. The Car Park Can you make reliable calls from your building's car park, loading bay, or outdoor areas?
5. Staff Workarounds Do employees use WhatsApp instead of calling clients? Walk to windows for signal? Complain about missed calls?

Your Score

0-1 checks: Minor issues — might be manageable

2-3 checks: Moderate problems — affecting productivity

4-5 checks: Significant dead zones — definitely fixable

Why Your Office Has Dead Zones

It's not your mobile network. It's your building.

Modern offices are designed to be energy-efficient and secure. Unfortunately, the same materials that keep heat in and unauthorised visitors out also block mobile signals:

The Signal Killers

  • Concrete and steel frames — The structural backbone of most modern offices, impenetrable to radio waves
  • Low-E glass windows — Energy-efficient coatings that reflect heat... and mobile signals
  • Metal cladding and insulation — Creates a Faraday cage effect
  • Underground basements — Earth and concrete block signals completely

Why WiFi Calling Isn't the Answer

Many IT managers suggest WiFi calling as a workaround. Here's why it falls short:

  • Inconsistent handover: Calls drop when moving between WiFi and mobile networks
  • Emergency calls: WiFi calling may not support emergency services properly
  • Visitor experience: Clients and guests don't have your WiFi password
  • Professional image: "Can you hear me now?" doesn't inspire confidence

The Fix (And What to Avoid)

❌ Don't Waste Time On:

  • Switching mobile providers — All networks use the same frequencies. If one doesn't work, none will.
  • Consumer signal boosters from Amazon — Illegal in the UK, interfere with emergency services, Ofcom can fine you.
  • Asking staff to "just use WhatsApp" — Unprofessional, doesn't solve the root problem.
